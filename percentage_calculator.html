What is a percentage?

In mathematics, a percentage is a number or ratio that represents a fraction of 100. It is one of the ways to represent a dimensionless relationship between two numbers; other methods include ratios, fractions, and decimals. Percentages are often denoted by the symbol "%" written after the number. They can also be denoted by writing "percent" or "pct" after the number. For example, 35% is equivalent to the decimal 0.35, or the fractions 35/100 and 7/20.

Percentages are often used in various fields such as finance, economics, social sciences, and many more. They are used to express a relationship between two quantities. Percentages can be used to compare different quantities, to express a proportion of a whole, or to express a share of a total. For example, if a company has a 75% market share, it means that 75% of the market is occupied by that company. Percentages are also used in graphical representations, such as progress bars or pie charts, to show the proportion of a whole.

Percentages are useful because they simplify ratios and dimensions. For example, a 500% increase is the same as a 5x increase. A 50% reduction is the same as a 50/100 reduction, or a 1/2 reduction.

Percentages can be represented in decimal form (0.20) or fractional form (1/5). They can also be represented in a mixture of both, like 35.625.
