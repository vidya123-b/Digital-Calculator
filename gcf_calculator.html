What is the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)?

The Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is the largest positive integer that divides both of the numbers without leaving a remainder. For example, if the numbers are 12 and 24, the GCF is 12. If the numbers are 24 and 36, the GCF is 12.

Prime Factorization Method

Prime Factorization Method is a method used to calculate the GCF. The method involves dividing each number by its smallest prime factor until a number less than the other is obtained, and then repeating the process with the resulting numbers.

Example:

  • GCF(16, 88, 104)
    • 16 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2
    • 88 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 11
    • 104 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 13
    • GCF(16, 88, 104) = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8

Euclidean Algorithm

The Euclidean Algorithm is another method used to calculate the GCF. The algorithm involves repeatedly dividing the two numbers by their greatest common divisor until they become equal.

Example:

  • GCF(16, 88)
    • Step 1: Divide 88 by 16, quotient is 5, remainder 0
    • Step 2: Divide 16 by 0, quotient is 16, remainder 0 (GCF is 16)
