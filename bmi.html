BMI Calculator




BMI Weight Category Health Risk
Below 18.5 Underweight Increased risk of early death, osteoporosis, impaired fertility, etc.
18.5 - 24.9 Healthy weight Low risk of health problems
25.0 - 29.9 Overweight Increased risk of developing health problems such as type 2 diabetes, heart disease, stroke, certain types of cancer, etc.
30.0 and above Obese Increased risk of serious health problems.
It is important to note that BMI is a general guideline and may not apply to everyone.
Here are some limitations of BMI:
Does not distinguish between muscle and fat mass:
People with a high muscle mass, such as athletes or bodybuilders, may have a high BMI even though they have a low body fat percentage. Does not account for body fat distribution: People who carry their weight around their waist (apple-shaped) are at a higher risk for health problems than people who carry their weight around their hips and thighs (pear-shaped).. May not be accurate for certain populations: BMI may not be as accurate for older adults, pregnant women, or people with very muscular builds or very slender builds.. If you are concerned about your weight or health, it is important to talk to your doctor. They can help you determine your healthy weight range and develop a plan to reach and maintain a healthy weight..
