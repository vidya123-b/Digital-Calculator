BMR is typically between 1,000 and 2,000 calories, meaning you need 1,000 to 2,000 calories to fuel basic functions


BMR is based on your height, weight, age, sex, and genetics. For example, men's BMR tends to be around 1600–1800 calories a day, while most women have a BMR of around 1550 calories a day. However, even if you and your workout buddy are the same age, sex, height, weight, and body composition, you still can have different BMRs. Things totally out of your control, like genetics and even organ size, impact BMR.

Factors affecting BMR:

 Body composition: Muscle mass burns more calories than fat tissue, so individuals with higher muscle mass tend to have a higher BMR.
Age: BMR generally declines with age as muscle mass naturally decreases.
Gender: Men typically have a higher BMR than women due to their larger muscle mass.
Height: Taller individuals tend to have a higher BMR than shorter individuals due to their larger body surface area.
Genetics: Some individuals are genetically predisposed to having a higher or lower BMR.
