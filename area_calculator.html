Area

The area of a shape is the measure of the amount of space inside the shape. It is calculated by multiplying the length of the base by the length of the height. This is particularly useful in calculating the area of a rectangle, square, circle, ellipse, triangle, and more.

Rectangle Area

For a rectangle, the area is the length times the width.

Formula:
Area = Width (w) x Height (h)

Example:
Width (w) = 30
Height (h) = 20
Area = 30 x 20 = 600

Triangle Area

For a triangle, the area is the half of the base times the height.

Formula:
Area = 0.5 x Base (b) x Height (h)

Example:
Base (b) = 30
Height (h) = 20
Area = 0.5 x 30 x 20 = 600

Trapezoid Area

For a trapezoid, the area is the sum of the bases multiplied by the height divided by two.

Formula:
Area = (Base 1 (b1) + Base 2 (b2)) x Height (h) / 2

Example:
Base 1 (b1) = 30
Base 2 (b2) = 20
Height (h) = 25
Area = (30 + 20) x 25 / 2 = 625

Circle Area

For a circle, the area is the area of a circle with the radius as its diameter.

Formula:
Area = Π x Radius (r) x Radius (r)

Example:
Radius (r) = 30
Area = Π x 30 x 30 = 2826.85

Sector Area

For a sector, the area is the area of the circle formed by the center of the sector and the radius.

Formula:
Area = Π x Radius (r) x Radius (r) / 2 - (Π x Radius (r) x Radius (r) / 2) * (cos(theta))

Example:
Radius (r) = 30
Area = Π x 30 x 30 / 2 - (Π x 30 x 30 / 2) * (cos(45)) = 2826.85

Ellipse Area

For an ellipse, the area is the area of the ellipse.

Formula:
Area = Π x a x b

Example:
a = 30
b = 20
Area = Π x 30 x 20 = 2826.85

Parallelogram Area

For a parallelogram, the area is the area of the parallelogram.

Formula:
Area = base (b) x height (h)

Example:
base (b) = 30
height (h) = 20
Area = 30 x 20 = 600
