What is the Least Common Multiple (LCM)?

The least common multiple (LCM) of two or more integers is the smallest positive integer that is a multiple of all of the integers. In other words, it's the smallest number that can be divided evenly by all of the given numbers.

Brute Force Method

In the Brute Force Method, we simply check all numbers from 1 to the product of the two inputs, and see which one is the least common multiple. This method can be slow and inefficient, especially for larger numbers.

For example, let's find the LCM of 18 and 26. Here's how we can do it:

  • 18: 18, 36, 54, 72, 90, 108, 126, 144, 162, 180, 198, 216, 234
  • 26: 26, 52, 78, 104, 130, 156, 182, 208, 234

We can see that the smallest common multiple of 18 and 26 is 234.
